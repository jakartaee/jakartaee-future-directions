# Jakarta EE Future Directions Meeting Summary — October 2, 2025

**Source:** Transcript file `Jakarta EE Future Directions - i20251002.docx`

## Summary

The group used this meeting to brainstorm future topics for the Jakarta EE Future Directions Interest Group and discuss how to attract more participation. Neil Patterson noted that without concrete topics it is difficult to draw attendees, and Michael Redlich reviewed several candidate topics already being tracked.

## Key Discussion Points

### Future Topic Planning

The meeting focused primarily on identifying useful future discussion topics for the interest group. Participants agreed that having clearer, scheduled topics would help improve attendance and engagement.

### EU Cyber Resilience Act

Steve Butler proposed a discussion on the EU Cyber Resilience Act and its implications for open source usage in commercial products. The group agreed this would be a valuable topic, especially for Jakarta EE vendors and organizations building products on open source libraries and runtimes.

Steve offered to lead a future discussion once his schedule allows.

### Jakarta Portlet and Jakarta Portlet Bridge

Michael Redlich raised Jakarta Portlet and Jakarta Portlet Bridge as possible future topics. The discussion would likely focus on their migration from Java EE to Jakarta EE, current contributors, and the role of Liferay in bringing those specifications forward.

### MicroProfile Config and Jakarta Config

The group discussed MicroProfile Config moving toward Jakarta Config. Emily Jiang was identified as the likely person to provide the latest status when ready.

### Standalone Specifications and Platform Inclusion

Jared Anderson clarified how standalone specifications relate to the Jakarta EE platform. Some specifications, such as MVC, XML Web Services, XML Binding, Portlet, and potentially NoSQL, may continue evolving independently without necessarily becoming part of the Platform, Web Profile, or Core Profile.

Vendors may still support these standalone technologies even when they are not included in the platform.

### Jakarta Debugging and Jakarta Pages

The group discussed Jakarta Debugging as a possible future topic, including whether it should be folded into Jakarta Pages. Jared noted that Jakarta Pages is currently the only known user of Jakarta Debugging, and the group considered whether a broader Jakarta EE debugging story would be useful.

### Other Potential Topics

Additional possible topics included:

- Jakarta Query
- Jakarta NoSQL
- Jakarta MVC
- Jakarta Security consolidation
- Possible folding of Jakarta Authorization and Jakarta Authentication under Jakarta Security
